WHAT IS EAP?

EAP stands for English for Academic Purposes. It is a specialized branch of English language education designed specifically for university students and researchers. Unlike everyday casual English, EAP focuses entirely on the formal, structured, and objective language required to succeed in a university environment. It trains you to master critical skills, such as writing research papers, understanding scientific journals, and delivering professional presentations using precise terminology.

Core Rules of EAP

To write professionally in an academic environment, you must follow these four fundamental rules:

  • 1. Use Formal Tone

    Avoid using informal words, slang, or shortcuts. Every word must be written in its full form.

    Example: Write "do not" instead of "don't".
  • 2. Maintain Objectivity

    Focus entirely on facts, data, and evidence. Avoid using personal pronouns like "I", "We", or "In my opinion".

    Example: Write "The data shows..." instead of "I think...".
  • 3. Apply Hedging (Cautious Language)

    Do not make absolute claims unless they are 100% proven. Use cautious language to show academic humility.

    Example: Use words like "suggests", "could", or "may".
  • 4. Use Passive Voice

    Shift the focus from the person doing the action to the action itself to make the writing sound more scientific.

    Example: Write "The experiment was conducted".
